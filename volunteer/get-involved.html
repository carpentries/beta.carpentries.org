CarpentryCon Committee Member

Our biennial conference, CarpentryCon is an opportunity for our community to come together and share knowledge. We have had both virtual and in-person iterations of the event, which requires collaboration between the Carpentries Core Team and our community. The goal of the Committee members is to make that collaboration possible.

CarpentryCon Committee members work on logistical support for the event including communications, programming, and accessibility. Committee members are divided into roles for these specific tasks and meet regularly before the event to ensure smooth operation.

Able to dedicate sufficient time to role.

Varies. Estimates include:
  • In the 12 months before the event: 2+ hours per month
  • In the 6 months before the event: 4+ hours per month
  • Attending the event and providing support as needed

CarpentryCon is held every two years. Recruitment occurs a minimum of 18 months in advance for an in-person event and twelve months in advance for a virtual event.

  • If you are intersted in helping to organise the next event please email carpentrycon@carpentries.org to inform them of your interest, and they will notify you when volunteer recruitment begins.

Workshop Helper

A Helper is a volunteer who joins a workshop to assist the Instructors. Helpers monitor the room and assist learners who may need help with troubleshooting or have questions.

  • monitoring the room for learners who might need help or additional support
  • help learners with troubleshooting
  • assist during breakout activities
  • responding to questions
  • assist Instructors by passing out materials or other items as needed

  • Familiarity with the tools/platforms being taught in the workshop
  • Able to attend the workshop

Flexible: time will vary depending on how many workshops you sign up to be a helper.

Each workshop is approximately 16 hours (Two 8-hour days or Four 4-hour days).

  • Email workshops@carpentries.org to share your interest and find out about opportunities.

  • More exposure and experience with the tools being taught at the workshop
  • Experience with Carpentries workshops that help you to become and Instructor and possibly lead your own workshops
