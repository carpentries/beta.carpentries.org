Overview

Instructor Trainers (Trainers) teach The Carpentries Instructor Training Events. They train new Instructors in the basics of educational psychology and evidence-based teaching practices. They also support and play a leadership role in the oversight of the Instructor Training program.

Instructor Trainer Training is the process by which we onboard new Instructor Trainers. The training consists of 10 weekly 1-hour sessions, where we will discuss The Carpentries teaching philosophy and review supporting materials summarising the research that underpins our Instructor Training curriculum.

How To Become A Trainer

The Carpentries Trainer Training is a highly-competitive training program that is openly available to everyone. A limited number of applicants are accepted into the program each year. Training is run 1-2 times per year and takes place over 10 weekly 1-hour sessions. Following the 10-week Training, trainees are asked to complete a number of exercises to become certified Carpentries Trainers.

  1. Submit An Application & Get Accepted
    Applications for Trainer Training open 1-2 times per year. You will need to submit an application and be accepted into the program to attend training.
  2. Complete 10 Week Training
    Attend 10 weekly 1-hour sessions where you'll learn the basics of educational psychology and evidence-based teaching practices, and how to teach this information to others.
  3. Complete Certification Steps
    After the training, you will be asked to shadow an Instructor Training Event and a Teaching Demonstration.

Prior Instructor Certification

If you are not a certified Carpentries Instructor, please expect a larger time committment when becoming a certified Trainer. While being a certified Carpentries Instructor is not a pre-requisite to being accepted in the Trainer Training program, we do require that all Trainers be certified Carpentries Instructors. If you are not a certified Carpentries Instructor before being accepted into the Trainer Training program, you will be required to complete the Instructor Training Program and become a certified Instructor for your after training certification steps.

If you are not a certified Carpentries Instructor and you are interested in becoming a Trainer, we encourage you to become an Instructor while you wait for Trainer Training applications to open.

Maintaining Trainer Certification

Trainer Certrification is renewed annually and is based on the Trainer's activity in the previous year. Active Trainer participation is important to maintaining Trainer Certification. Trainers can maintain their active certification status by participating in the following activities as their schedule allows:

  • Teaching Instructor Training
  • Hosting Teaching Demonstrations
  • Attending Monthly Trainer Community Meetings
