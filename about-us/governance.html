Introduction

The governing body of the The Carpentries is the Executive Council. The nine member Council includes representation from The Carpentries community and others, with four elected positions by the community, and five appointed from the Executive Council.

Roles and Responsibilities of the Executive Council

As the highest leadership body of The Carpentries, The Executive Council is responsible for:

  • Strategic and organisational planning
  • Financial oversight
  • Hiring and performance of the Excecutive Director
  • Approval and assessment of The Carpentries programs and services
  • Advocacy

The Executive Council executes these responsibilities through a combination of quarterly Executive Council meetings and regular correspondence and collaboration via email and online platforms.

Strategic Plan

The Carpentries Strategic Plan was developed and approved by the Executive Council, with support from the Core Team, in January 2020. The Strategic Plan lays out six broad goals, each with its rationale and underlying objectives, that will serve as touchpoints to guide our future actions. The idea is to provide a general framework for the direction of this organisation for the next 3-5 years.

Contacting the Executive Council

If you have a question or concern for the Executive Council (EC), there are three ways to contact members of The Executive Council:

  • File an issue in the EC's repository. This is appropriate if you would like to solicit additional community feedback on a topic of general interest to the community.
  • Send us an email. This is appropriate for communicating directly with the Executive Council but not the rest of our community.
  • Submit a feedback form. This is preferable if you would like confidential communication with the Executive Council, or if you would like to remain anonymous.
